# Structure Contract: Primary Market Industry Research Report

## Two Structure Patterns

### Pattern A: Deep Discussion (TMT / Tech / Opinion-Heavy)

TMT / tech opinion-heavy deep-dive format

**Characteristics:**
- No formal Table of Contents
- Numbered chapters: 01, 02, 03, 04, 05, 06... in dark-red KaiTi
- Each chapter is a broad thesis area
- Subsections use plain headings without numbering, in dark-red KaiTi
- Text analysis with supporting native charts and data exhibits
- Benchmark comparison tables as primary exhibits for AI/model research
- Native charts for market sizing, competitive positioning, technology trends, and financial data
- Executive summary on first page after title block (in bordered paragraph)
- English source quotes in italic centered blocks

**Typical Section Flow:**

```
Cover Page
  [White background | Logo top-right (icon + institution name bilingual)
   | Title in bold KaiTi left-aligned | Author (Cr.: name) | Date | QR code]

Executive Summary (~1 page)
  [Bordered/quoted paragraph block with thesis overview]

01 [Core Question / Thesis Statement — dark-red KaiTi bold heading]
  [Subsection: Key evidence or argument — dark-red KaiTi bold]
  [Subsection: Supporting analysis — dark-red KaiTi bold]
  [Data table, benchmark comparison, or native chart]

02 [Competitive Dynamic — dark-red KaiTi bold heading]
  [Subsection: Player A analysis — dark-red KaiTi bold]
  [Subsection: Player B analysis — dark-red KaiTi bold]
  [Chart, data table, or case study — with caption]

03 [Technology / Product Trend]
  ...

04 [Commercialization Landscape]
  ...

05 [Investment & Financing Landscape — dark-red KaiTi bold heading]
  [Subsection: Funding round timeline and key deals — dark-red KaiTi bold]
  [Subsection: Investor mapping and capital flow trends — dark-red KaiTi bold]
  [Chart: funding timeline, deal size distribution, or investor landscape map]

06 [Future Outlook / Predictions]
  ...

07 [Concluding Thoughts / Investment Implications]
  ...
```

**Example chapter structure (TMT deep-dive):**
- 01 核心问题：[产品/技术]为什么值得关注?
- 02 竞争格局：行业玩家与领先态势
- 03 技术趋势：关键技术突破与演进路径
- 04 基础设施：底层能力与系统级优势
- 05 投融资全景：一级市场资金流向与关键交易
- 06 商业化前瞻：变现路径与市场机会
- 07 展望：下一代产品形态与投资含义

### Pattern B: Traditional Sector Study (Consumer / Healthcare / Industrials)

Consumer / healthcare / industrials data-heavy sector study format

**Characteristics:**
- Formal Table of Contents (目录) with 3-level numbering, dot-leaders, page numbers
- Hierarchical numbering: 1, 1.1, 1.1.1, 2, 2.1... in black KaiTi
- Data-heavy with frequent exhibits (figures and tables)
- Cover page + TOC (page 2-3) before content begins
- Running header with report title + horizontal rule on all content pages
- Multiple data visualization types (pie, bar, line charts)
- Detailed company/product profiles with financial metrics
- Industry chain analysis with flow diagrams

**Typical Section Flow:**

```
Cover Page
  [White background | Centered title | Centered logo | Centered institution name
   | Centered date (YYYY.MM)]
```

## Exhibit Numbering & Anchoring

### Figures (图)
- Sequential numbering within the report: 图 1, 图 2, 图 3...
- Caption format: "图 N  [Description]" (space between number and description)
- Position: Above the figure (Style B) or below (Style A)
- Source note: "数据来源：[source]" below caption
- Style B: two figures may share a row with individual captions

### Tables (表)
- Sequential numbering, separate from figures: 表 1, 表 2, 表 3...
- Caption format: "表 N  [Description]" (space between number and description)
- Position: Above the table
- Source note: "数据来源：[source]" or "资料来源：[source]" below table

### Cross-References
- In text: "如图 N 所示" or "见表 N"
- Near the referenced exhibit, not pages away

## Source Attribution Requirements

Every report must include source attribution for:
1. **Market size / TAM data**: Government statistics, industry association reports
2. **Financial data**: Company filings, public disclosures
3. **Consumer data**: Survey reports, white papers
4. **Technical data**: Research papers, patent filings
5. **Competitive data**: Public company reports, market research firms
6. **Investment & financing data**: Crunchbase, PitchBook, CB Insights, IT桔子, 烯牛数据, 天眼查, public deal announcements, IPO prospectuses

### Preferred Source Hierarchy
1. Government databases (e.g., national statistics bureaus, commerce departments, USDA, Eurostat)
2. Industry associations and regulatory bodies
3. Public company filings (annual reports, prospectuses, SEC/HKEx/SSE filings)
4. Reputable research institutions (investment bank research, consulting firms)
5. Authoritative media (The Information, Bloomberg, Reuters, Financial Times)
6. Company official disclosures

## Content Quality Standards

### For Pattern A (Deep Discussion)
- Each chapter presents a clear argument or thesis
- Evidence is analytical, not just descriptive
- Forward-looking predictions with reasoning
- Comparison across companies/platforms/products with supporting charts (bar, radar, or comparison tables)
- Market sizing and growth trends visualized with native line/bar charts
- Competitive landscape illustrated with market share charts or positioning maps
- Technology comparison via benchmark tables (especially for AI/model research: accuracy, latency, cost, parameters)
- Investment & financing review with key deals, round sizes, investor profiles, and capital flow narrative
- Embedded quotes from industry insiders or executives
- English text blocks for international sources (italic, centered, with "source:" prefix)
- Strong opinions backed by reasoning and quantitative evidence, not just neutral description

### For Pattern B (Traditional Sector)
- Each section is data-grounded with specific numbers
- Market size with historical trend and forecast where possible
- Competitive landscape with market share quantification (CR3, CR5, etc.)
- Value chain analysis with margin assessment at each stage
- Consumer behavior backed by survey data with percentages
- Company profiles with specific financial metrics (revenue, sales volume, market share)
- Investment & financing landscape with: key deal table (date, company, round, amount, lead investors), annual funding volume trend, investor mapping, IPO/exit events, and capital flow narrative
- Risk factors clearly articulated where relevant

## Page Break Rules

- Cover page: standalone, no header/footer
- TOC: standalone (may span 2 pages for long reports), no running header on first TOC page (Style A only; Style B has header)
- New chapter: always page break before
- Large exhibits: may span full page width
- Tables exceeding page width: landscape orientation or split across pages
- Charts: may appear side-by-side on same row when related
